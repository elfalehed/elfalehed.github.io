To try and encapsulate the reasons behind building in public, spending hours tweaking this open-sourced Jekyll theme, coming up with ideas for a livestream that's going to get three people watching at best, or even spending months in a country that a random tourist can discover more of in three days than I did in three months, is an attempt to guide you, the viewer, toward seeing what I believe matters.

The goal behind everything we do across our portfolio of projects is to create solutions and products with a clear outcome in mind. It was never focused on material gain alone, but also on the passion of tackling a hard problem for the sake of it, growing with it, and becoming a better human through the process. Building was never the end goal; it has always been the mechanism through which transformation happens. Every project, every tool, and every meaningful pursuit shapes you into someone who is free of labels.

You're the architect, the software developer, the financial analyst with venture-building experience, running the sales side of a mathematically rigorous and scientifically grounded project. You enjoy the process, you grow with it, and the end goal is complete transformation. Those are also the kind of people I want to have around.
